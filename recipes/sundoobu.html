Sundoobu Recipe

Sundoobu is a comforting Korean soup that warms the soul and delights the taste buds. It features soft tofu and a variety of vegetables, creating a flavorful and satisfying combination. Whether enjoyed on a chilly day or as a nourishing meal, Sundoobu brings joy to every spoonful.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups vegetable broth
  • 8 ounces soft tofu, cubed
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon gochugaru (Korean red pepper flakes)
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1/2 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 medium zucchini, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 medium carrot, sliced
  • 4-5 shiitake mushrooms, sliced
  • 2 green onions, chopped
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. In a pot, heat vegetable broth over medium heat.
  2. Add the sliced onions, minced garlic, and sliced shiitake mushrooms to the pot. Cook until the vegetables soften.
  3. Add the sliced zucchini and carrots to the pot. Cook for a few more minutes.
  4. Add the soy sauce, gochugaru, and sesame oil to the pot. Stir well to combine.
  5. Gently add the cubed tofu to the pot. Be careful not to break it.
  6. Simmer the soup for about 5-7 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld together.
  7. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  8. Garnish with chopped green onions.
  9. Serve the Sundoobu hot and enjoy!

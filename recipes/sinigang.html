Sinigang Recipe

Sinigang is a beloved Filipino soup known for its tangy and savory flavors. It is typically made with tamarind as the souring agent and filled with a variety of vegetables and protein. Whether enjoyed on a rainy day or as a comforting meal, Sinigang never fails to satisfy the taste buds and bring warmth to the heart.

Ingredients

  • 8 cups of water
  • 1 pound pork ribs or beef short ribs
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 3 medium potatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish, peeled and sliced
  • 1 cabbage, sliced
  • 1 long green chili pepper
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach) or spinach
  • 1 cup string beans, cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 2 tablespoons tamarind paste
  • Fish sauce or salt to taste

Steps

  1. In a large pot, bring water to a boil.
  2. Add the pork ribs or beef short ribs to the boiling water. Cook until tender and skim off any scum that rises to the surface.
  3. Add the quartered onion and potatoes to the pot. Simmer for a few minutes until the flavors infuse into the broth.
  4. Add the sliced radish and cabbage to the pot. Cook until the vegetables start to soften.
  5. Add the long green chili pepper, kangkong or spinach, and string beans to the pot. Cook for a few more minutes.
  6. Dissolve the tamarind paste in a small amount of water, then add it to the pot. Stir well to incorporate the sour flavor.
  7. Season the soup with fish sauce or salt to taste.
  8. Simmer the Sinigang for a few more minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  9. Remove from heat and serve the Sinigang hot with steamed rice.

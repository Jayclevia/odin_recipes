Kimbap Recipe

Kimbap (김밥), a traditional Korean dish, is a nostalgic childhood favorite. It is a delightful combination of rice and dried seaweed sheets, filled with a variety of tasty ingredients. Whether enjoyed on school trips, family outings, or prepared by loving parents, these portable rice rolls hold a special place in our hearts, evoking memories of cherished moments.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups uncooked short grain rice
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • salt to taste
  • 8 ounces lean tender beef
  • 2 teaspoons soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon rice wine (or mirin)
  • 1 teaspoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon minced garlic
  • 1 bunch spinach
  • 1 teaspoon sesame oil
  • 2 medium carrots
  • 5 yellow pickled radish
  • sheets of seaweed

Steps

  1. Cook rice with slightly less water than usual.
  2. Cut beef into thin strips and stir-fry with seasoning until cooked.
  3. Blanch spinach, cool in ice bath, and season with sesame oil and salt.
  4. Julienne carrots and stir-fry until softened, season lightly with salt.
  5. Cut pickled radish into strips.
  6. Beat eggs with salt, cook in a pan, and cut into strips.
  7. Cut fish cake into strips, stir-fry with soy sauce, sugar, and sesame oil until softened.
  8. Arrange all ingredients on a plate.
  9. Season hot rice with sesame oil and salt.
  10. Place rice on a gim sheet, add fillings, and roll tightly.
  11. Brush roll with sesame oil and cut into bite-sized pieces.
